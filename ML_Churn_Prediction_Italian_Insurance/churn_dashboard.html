The Business Story
01 — Problem
1 in 5 clients disappears
21.47% of the auto insurance customer base fails to renew each year — 161,158 clients lost per cycle, representing direct premium revenue loss and costly new-client acquisition spend.
02 — Approach
7 ML experiments, WandB tracked
Leakage-free models across LightGBM, XGBoost, Random Forest and Logistic Regression. Two feature strategies: 14 features with engineered Total_Warranties vs. 18 features with raw coverage flags. All runs logged to WandB.

Data Leakage Prevention
🚫 Status Removed
Direct churn label encoder — leakage
Contract Status (Sostituito, Sospensione, etc.) directly encodes whether a contract was renewed or cancelled — a near-perfect proxy for CHURN. Kept it inflated accuracy to ~92% artificially. Removed in this pipeline.
🚫 3 Date Columns Removed
NEXT_CONTRACT_DATE, WINDOW_END, EARLY_TERMINATION
These columns are directly derived from the churn label construction logic. Including them allows the model to trivially reconstruct the target — removed to ensure the model learns real behavioural patterns.
✅ Number_of_Accidents Added
Genuine behavioural risk signal
Accidents correlate with claim frequency and premium increases — both drivers of churn. This is a real behavioural signal (not leakage) that improves model calibration for high-risk customers.

⚠ Why precision / F1 / accuracy dropped on full dataset:
The model uses class_weight='balanced' — it is deliberately tuned to catch as many churners as possible (92.7% recall). On the full 750K dataset with real class imbalance (78.5% loyal vs 21.5% churn), this means more false positives, reducing precision from ~83% to ~43.5%.

This is the correct trade-off for retention campaigns — catching 92.7% of churners (150K clients) is worth some over-flagging. The ROC-AUC improvement (+0.003) confirms the full-data model actually ranks customers better.

Training Strategy
Why 30% sample for search, then 100% for production
RandomizedSearchCV with 5-fold CV on 750K rows would take hours. Using 30% (~225K rows) preserves the churn distribution (stratified) while reducing search time by 70%. Final production model then trains on all 750K with best params — confirmed by ROC-AUC improvement from 0.884 → 0.8869.

3-Tier Retention Strategy
🔴 Tier 1 — High Risk
Churn probability ≥ 75% · ~40K clients
Dedicated Account Manager Call
Personal outreach 90 days before expiry. Offer loyalty discount (10–15%) or premium restructuring. Flag for immediate escalation.
Personalised Retention Bundle
Bundle additional coverage (road assistance, kasko) at reduced rate. Target clients with 0 warranties — high churn correlation.
🟡 Tier 2 — Medium Risk
Churn probability 50–75% · ~60K clients
Automated Renewal Campaign
Email/SMS sequence 60 days before expiry. Highlight loyalty benefits. Early renewal discount (5%). No manual agent involvement.
Cross-Sell Opportunity
Offer complementary home or health insurance at renewal. Clients with multiple products have significantly lower churn rates.
🟢 Tier 3 — Low Risk
Churn probability < 50% · ~450K clients
Loyalty Maintenance Programme
Annual satisfaction survey + NPS tracking. Automated anniversary message with discount voucher. No proactive intervention needed.
Referral Programme
Engaged loyal clients are the best advocates. Referral bonus (€50 premium credit) for successful new client introductions.
